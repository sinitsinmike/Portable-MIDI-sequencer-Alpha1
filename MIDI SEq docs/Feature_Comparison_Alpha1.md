
# Feature Comparison — Hardware MIDI Sequencers (Budget Segment)

## Devices Compared
- Portable MIDI Sequencer — Alpha 1
- Arturia KeyStep
- Arturia KeyStep Pro
- Korg Volca (generic sequencer use)
- Korg SQ-64

## Core MIDI Sequencing Capabilities

| Feature | Alpha 1 | KeyStep | KeyStep Pro | Korg Volca | Korg SQ-64 |
|--------|---------|---------|-------------|------------|------------|
| Pure MIDI sequencer | Yes | No | No | No | Partial |
| Live MIDI recording | Yes | Limited | Limited | Limited | Limited |
| Multi-track sequencing | 16 tracks | 1 | 4 | 1 | 4 |
| 16 MIDI channels | Yes | No | Partial | No | Partial |
| External instruments | Up to 16 | No | Limited | No | Limited |
| Project save/load | Yes | No | No | No | Limited |
| Standalone (no computer) | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |

## Workflow & Interaction

| Feature | Alpha 1 | KeyStep | KeyStep Pro | Korg Volca | Korg SQ-64 |
|--------|---------|---------|-------------|------------|------------|
| Performance-first workflow | Yes | Partial | Partial | No | Partial |
| Linear recording | Yes | Partial | Partial | No | Partial |
| Loop recording | Yes | Partial | Partial | No | Partial |
| Step/grid sequencing | No | Partial | Partial | Yes | Yes |
| Minimal screen gazing | Yes | Yes | Partial | Partial | Partial |

## Connectivity & Hardware

- MIDI TRS In / Out
- 5-pin DIN via adapter
- Internal clock
- MIDI Clock In / Out — Planned
- 0.96" OLED display
- 8 LEDs for visual feedback

## Price Positioning

- Alpha 1: $100–150
- KeyStep: ~$100
- KeyStep Pro: ~$350
- Korg Volca: ~$150–200
- Korg SQ-64: ~$200–250

## Key Takeaway

Alpha 1 occupies a unique position as a budget, performance-oriented,
multi-track, multi-channel hardware MIDI sequencer designed for
computer-free, hands-on music creation.
